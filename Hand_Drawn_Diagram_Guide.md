# Hand-Drawn Diagram Concept & Design Guide

The assignment requires you to **draw a diagram by hand on paper** (using pen/pencil) showing how three forces interact: **Psychology, Technology, and Business**. 

Below is a detailed layout guide, a structural representation, and the exact labels and connections to draw. 

---

## 1. Visual Layout of the Page
To fit everything on one page cleanly, structure your page in a **triangle layout**:

```
                  [ PSYCHOLOGY ]
               (The Customer's Mind)
                 /             \
    (Trust & Price)           (CTR & Conversions
     determines CVR            feed Algorithm)
           /                     \
          v                       v
     [ BUSINESS ] <=========> [ TECHNOLOGY ]
  (The Brand's P&L)   (Bids &   (Amazon Ad Platform)
                       Data)
```

---

## 2. Core Elements to Draw in Each Box

Sketch three large boxes (or clouds) on your paper and write the following bullet points inside them:

### Box A: Psychology (Customer's Mind)
* **Search Intent**: Is the customer looking for a specific solution (e.g., "premium face serum") or browsing?
* **Attention Filter**: Customers ignore irrelevant ads. They click what matches their eye.
* **Visual Trust**: Star ratings, reviews, and high-quality lifestyle images build trust.
* **Friction & Price Sensitivity**: High prices or delivery fees cause customers to abandon the cart.

### Box B: Technology (Amazon Ad Platform & Algorithm)
* **Ad Auction Engine**: Instantly matches keyword bids + "Quality Score" (CTR & CVR) to decide who gets shown at the top.
* **Organic Search Index**: The ranking algorithm. Paid sales signal to Amazon that the product is popular, boosting organic ranking.
* **Data Pipelines**: Tracking clicks, spend, conversions, and mapping them to report metrics (ROAS, ACOS).

### Box C: Business (Brand P&L & Decisions)
* **Unit Economics**: Selling Price (SP) minus Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) equals Gross Margin.
* **Margin Leaks**: Amazon Referral Fees (12%) and Ad Cost per Unit eat into the margins.
* **Organic Baseline**: Organic sales have zero ad cost and represent pure profit margin.
* **Management Decisions**: Set budgets, adjust selling prices, optimize product pages, launch new channels.

---

## 3. Labeled Connections (Arrows) to Draw

Draw arrows connecting the boxes and write the following labels on the arrows. This shows **systems thinking** rather than isolated boxes:

### Connecting Psychology & Technology
1. **Arrow from Psychology to Technology (Label: "User Behavior Data Feed")**:
   * *Detail to write near arrow*: High click-through rate (CTR) and conversion rate (CVR) feed the tech engine, raising the listing's **Quality Score** and lowering the bidding cost (CPC) in auctions.
2. **Arrow from Technology to Psychology (Label: "Attention Capture")**:
   * *Detail to write near arrow*: The ad algorithm placements (Top of Search vs. Product Detail Pages) dictate what the user sees, shaping their buying choice.

### Connecting Technology & Business
3. **Arrow from Technology to Business (Label: "Performance Reporting & SEO Halo")**:
   * *Detail to write near arrow*: Tech outputs ROAS, ACOS, and search volumes. Paid sales volume triggers the Amazon search index, raising organic ranking and driving **free organic sales**.
4. **Arrow from Business to Technology (Label: "Operational Input")**:
   * *Detail to write near arrow*: The Brand Manager inputs budgets, target ACOS, and keyword bids into the platform, determining which tech auctions to enter.

### Connecting Business & Psychology
5. **Arrow from Business to Psychology (Label: "Trust & Value Signals")**:
   * *Detail to write near arrow*: Brand decisions on selling price, discount coupons, and listing images directly influence customer trust and purchase probability (reducing cart friction).
6. **Arrow from Psychology to Business (Label: "Revenue & Market Share")**:
   * *Detail to write near arrow*: Customer trust and transactional intent convert into sales volume, determining the brand's cash flow, margins, and market position.

---

## 4. Text Description of the "Connections" to Keep in Mind
When you write down these connections on paper, it shows the reviewer that you understand the system:
* **The Loop**: A brand manager sets a bid (**Business $\rightarrow$ Technology**). The algorithm shows the ad (**Technology $\rightarrow$ Psychology**). The customer trusts the listing and buys (**Psychology $\rightarrow$ Business**). This purchase feeds the conversion data back to the platform (**Psychology $\rightarrow$ Technology**), which improves the organic ranking (**Technology $\rightarrow$ Business**), leading to a lower TACOS and higher profits (**Business**).
